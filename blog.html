Email Security: Why It Matters for Australian Businesses

Email might seem like everyday business communication, but it's actually the number one way cybercriminals break into Australian companies.

Email is still the backbone of business communication — quotes, invoices, deals, and customer relationships all flow through email. Unfortunately, cybercriminals know this too. Today, email has become the number one entry point for cyberattacks.

Email: The Front Door to Your Business

Think of your email system like the front door of your company. If it's unlocked, anyone can walk in.

Cybercriminals commonly try phishing emails, CEO impersonation, domain spoofing, and credential theft. If they compromise even one account, the damage can escalate quickly.

SPF, DKIM & DMARC: Proving Your Emails Are Real

SPF tells the internet which systems can send email for your domain.

DKIM adds a digital signature like a tamper-proof seal.

DMARC tells servers what to do if authentication fails — monitor, quarantine, or reject.

Phishing-Resistant Authentication

Traditional MFA (SMS codes, authenticator apps) can still be tricked by sophisticated phishing.

Security Keys (FIDO2) require physical access — extremely difficult to phish.

Passwordless Passkeys use biometrics tied to legitimate websites — won't work on fake pages.

Advanced Threat Protection

Modern tools detect threats before they reach inboxes:

• Malicious attachments in secure sandboxes
• Dangerous links checked at click-time
• Impersonation attacks and lookalike domains

Security isn't "set and forget" — businesses constantly add new email systems. Continuous monitoring ensures your domain can't be spoofed, legitimate emails are delivered, and suspicious activity is detected early.
