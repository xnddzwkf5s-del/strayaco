Why Email Security Matters More Than Ever

And why your Australian business should care about protecting its most critical communication channel

Email remains the backbone of business communication — quotes, invoices, deals, and customer relationships all flow through it. Unfortunately, cybercriminals know this too. Today, email has become the number one entry point for cyberattacks.

Email: The Front Door to Your Business

Think of your email system like the front door of your company. If it's unlocked, anyone can walk in.

Cybercriminals commonly use tactics like:

  • Phishing emails to trick employees into revealing passwords
  • CEO impersonation to request urgent payments from finance staff
  • Domain spoofing to scam your customers or partners
  • Credential theft using stolen login information

If attackers compromise even one account, the damage can escalate quickly. That's why modern email protection needs more than just a spam filter — businesses need a layered security approach.

Key Insight: Email isn't just communication — it's your business's digital front door. Protecting it protects everything behind it.

Step One: Proving Your Emails Are Legitimate

Before worrying about attackers getting into your systems, there's another critical question: How do other mail servers know that emails from your company are real?

SPF – Who Can Send Email for Your Domain?

SPF (Sender Policy Framework) tells the internet which systems are allowed to send emails on behalf of your domain — like Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, CRM systems, or marketing platforms.

DKIM – The Digital Signature

DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail) works like a tamper-proof seal. Every outgoing email is digitally signed, confirming it came from your domain and hasn't been altered.

DMARC – The Bouncer at the Door

DMARC tells receiving servers what to do if authentication fails — monitor suspicious activity, send emails to spam, or completely reject fake messages pretending to be your company.

Step Two: Protecting Your Team from Phishing

Even with strong email authentication, attackers target your employees directly through convincing phishing emails.

Why Traditional MFA Isn't Always Enough

While SMS codes and authenticator apps help, sophisticated phishing attacks can still trick users into revealing both passwords and MFA codes.

The Rise of Phishing-Resistant Authentication

Security Keys (FIDO2)

Hardware keys that require physical access to authenticate — extremely difficult to phish and no shared passwords.

Passwordless Passkeys

Biometric authentication (fingerprint, face recognition) tied to legitimate websites — won't work on fake phishing pages.

Step Three: Stopping Threats Before They Reach the Inbox

Modern email security tools add another layer of defense:

Malicious Attachments
Suspicious files are opened in secure sandbox environments before reaching users.
Dangerous Links
Links are checked at the moment users click them, preventing access to harmful websites.
Impersonation Detection
Identifies fake CEO emails, display name spoofing, and lookalike domains designed to trick users.
Important: These scams are responsible for billions of dollars in fraud globally every year. Advanced protection isn't optional — it's essential.

Security Isn't "Set and Forget"

Even the best security setup needs ongoing monitoring. Businesses constantly add new systems that send email — marketing platforms, CRM tools, customer service systems, automation platforms.

Without proper monitoring, these changes can accidentally break authentication or create security gaps.

Most importantly, it protects your reputation and your customers.
